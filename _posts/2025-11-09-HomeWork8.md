## 2. The Shared Foundation â€” The Bernoulli Trial

A **Bernoulli trial** is a simple random experiment with two outcomes:
$$
X_i = 
\begin{cases}
1 & \text{with probability } q = 1-p \\
0 & \text{with probability } p
\end{cases}
$$

In the **Law of Large Numbers (LLN)** simulation, we observed:
$$
\bar{X}_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n X_i \to q \quad \text{as } n \to \infty
$$

In the **Server Random Walk simulation**, the focus shifts to:
$$
S_n = \sum_{i=1}^n Y_i, \quad Y_i = 2X_i - 1 \in \{+1, -1\}
$$
Thus,
$$
S_n = 2\sum_{i=1}^n X_i - n
$$

This direct transformation reveals the **deep algebraic connection**:
- The number of secure weeks $\( k = \sum X_i \)$ follows a **Binomial(n, q)** distribution.  
- The final score $\( S_n = 2k - n \)$ is a **linear rescaling** of that binomial variable.

## 3. Mathematical Properties and Connections

### (c) **Binomial Expansion**

The binomial theorem:
$$
(x + y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k} x^k y^{n-k}
$$

When we interpret $\( x = q \) and \( y = 1-q \)$:

And each term $\( \binom{n}{k} q^k (1-q)^{n-k} \)$ gives the **probability mass** for `k` secure weeks â€” i.e., the **binomial distribution** itself.

This expansion visually corresponds to **stacking all possible random walk trajectories** weighted by their probability.

### (d) **Combinatorial Interpretation**

Each trajectory of the random walk corresponds to one **sequence of outcomes**:
